Muhammad Ali

Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee!

Life Story:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1954 - His bicycle was stolen and he told the police officer, Joe Martin, that he wanted to "whoop" whoever did it. Martin trained young boxers and started to train Clay, who won his first fight and was declared the 'future world champ'
  • 1956 - Wins the novice Golden Gloves Championship as a light heavyweight
  • 1959 - Wins the AAU light heavyweight title at the Golden Gloves Tournament of Champions
  • 1960 - In September he wins the light heavyweight gold medal at the Rome Olympics, his first major success in athletics.
    In October he wins his first professional bout with Tunney Hunsaker
  • 1963 - Wins his first foreign professional bout with Henry Cooper in London before 55,000 fans
  • 1964 - Knocked out Sonny Liston. This defeat allowed him to become the heavyweight champion.
    He joined the Nation Of Islam shortly after. At the age of 22, he changed his name from Cassius Clay to Muhammad Ali
  • 1966 - Ali is drafted and he files for conscientious objector status as a Muslim minister
  • 1967 - In April, the government denies his filing for conscientious objector status and he was drafted into the Army. He attends the induction ceremony, but he refused to step forward.
    In June, Ali is found guilty of refusing induction into the Army. His passport is revoked, he is stripped of his heavyweight title and faces a 5 year prison sentence and a 3 year boxing ban
  • 1970 - Ali returns to fighting in Atlanta and wins over Jerry Quarry
  • 1971 - In March, in a famous match called "The Fight of the Century," Joe Frazier knocked Ali down, but Ali got up. However, after 15 brutal rounds, it was still ruled that Frazier won.
    In June, the Supreme Court reverses its 1967 conviction and ruled that he was not guilty of evading the military, since he should not have been drafted in the first place due to his religious beliefs
  • 1974 - In January, he won over Joe Frazier.
    In October, he defeated George Foreman and reclaimed the title of Heavyweight Champion of the World. This fight was a very famous event known as the "Rumble in the Jungle," since the fight was at a stadium in Zaire
  • 1975 - In September, Ali beats Ken Norton in Yankee Stadium in New York.
    In October, Ali beats Joe Frazier in Manila
  • 1978 - In February, Ali lost the heavyweight title to Leon Spinks in a split decision.
    In September, Ali wins the heavyweight title back from Leon Spinks after a battle at the Superdome in New Orleans
  • 1980 - Ali came back and fought with Harry Holmes. Ali lost the fight.
    Ali came back and fought with Trevor Berbick, once again, Ali lost the fight.
    Aged 39, Ali announces his retirement from boxing, diagnosed with parkinsons
  • 1996 - Ali lit the Olympic flame
  • 1997 - He is named Sportsman of the Century by Sports Illustrated
  • 2016 - After leaving the public eye, Muhammad Ali died on June 3 as the result of septic shock due to unspecified natural causes. He was 74 years old.
    He was buried in Louisville, Kentucky. He planned his own funeral which included eulogies from former President Bill Clinton, sportscaster Bryant Gumbel and comedian and close friend Billy Crystal

Publications

The Greatest: My Own Story, 1975. Many knew the man, but only he knew his life as he lived it. This is his autogbiography, written in collaboration with Richard Durham
The Soul of A Butterfly, 2003. A sincere & wholehearted read that provides comfort for difficult times, as Ali reflects on a variety of topics, from virtue to his personal relationship with God. An autobiography written with assistance from his daughter, Hana Yasmeen Ali.
Muhammad Ali Unfiltered: Rare, Iconic, and Officially Authorized Photos of the Greatest, 2016. An intimate look at the man, the boxer, the social activist, the iconic legend. A collection of unseen images, quotes and tributes compiled by his wife, Lonnie Ali.
